Biography

Tristan Rader grew up on Cleveland's Westside and built a career improving neighborhoods — from raising wages locally to reducing lead exposure and expanding green spaces. He served on City Council and now represents the community in the Statehouse.

Why Tristan's running

Tristan believes Ohio should be a place where working families can thrive, where clean water and parks are protected, and where public servants are accountable. His work focuses on energy reform, consumer protections, affordable housing, and stronger communities.

Community work

  • Led campaigns to raise local minimum wage and protect tenants.
  • Advocated for lead abatement and safer housing.
  • Organized neighborhood cleanups and park refurbishments.
